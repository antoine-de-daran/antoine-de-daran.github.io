Hello World: Why I'm Starting This Blog

March 11, 2026 · 3 min read

I've been reading technical blogs for years. Posts by people who take the time to explain how things actually work, not marketing copy disguised as content, but genuine write-ups from engineers who got their hands dirty. Those posts taught me more than most courses ever did. It's time I start giving back.

What to Expect

This blog will cover the topics I spend most of my time thinking about: machine learning, AI systems, and the engineering that holds it all together. Expect posts about:

  • How ML systems work under the hood, not just the API calls
  • Practical lessons from building and shipping real projects
  • Deep dives into papers, architectures, and paradigm shifts
  • Open-source tools and why I build them

Why Write?

Writing forces clarity. When you try to explain something, you quickly discover the gaps in your own understanding. I've found that the best way to truly learn a concept is to write about it as if someone else needs to understand it from scratch.

Beyond that, the ML/AI space moves fast. Keeping a written record of what I learn, what works, and what doesn't is as much for future-me as it is for anyone reading.

The Plan

I'll be writing here on a regular basis. No fixed schedule, no filler content to hit a quota. When I have something worth sharing, I'll write it up properly. Quality over frequency.

If you're interested in ML, AI, or just how complex systems work, stick around. And if you find something useful, consider supporting the effort.

Let's go.
